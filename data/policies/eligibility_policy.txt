GOVERNMENT SOCIAL SUPPORT PROGRAM - ELIGIBILITY POLICY DOCUMENT
================================================================

1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW
The Social Support Program provides financial assistance and economic enablement services
to residents who demonstrate genuine need for support. The program aims to help individuals
and families achieve financial stability and self-sufficiency.

2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

2.1 Income Assessment
- Monthly household income below AED 10,000 per month: HIGH PRIORITY
- Monthly household income between AED 10,000 - 20,000: MEDIUM PRIORITY
- Monthly household income above AED 20,000: LOW PRIORITY
- Per capita income (household income / family size) below AED 2,000: CRITICAL NEED
- Per capita income between AED 2,000 - 5,000: HIGH NEED
- Applicants with zero declared income require additional verification

2.2 Employment Status
- Unemployed individuals actively seeking work: HIGHEST PRIORITY
- Part-time employed individuals: HIGH PRIORITY
- Self-employed with inconsistent income: MEDIUM PRIORITY
- Fully employed individuals: assessed based on income adequacy only
- Employment history gaps exceeding 6 months warrant additional review

2.3 Family Size and Dependents
- Families with 5+ members: additional weight in assessment
- Single parents with dependents: HIGH PRIORITY
- Families with elderly dependents (65+): additional consideration
- Families with disabled dependents: additional consideration
- Number of school-age children affects support calculation

2.4 Wealth Assessment
- Net worth (assets minus liabilities) below AED 10,000: HIGH NEED
- Net worth between AED 10,000 - 50,000: MEDIUM NEED
- Net worth above AED 200,000: generally NOT eligible unless exceptional circumstances
- Debt-to-income ratio above 50%: indicates financial stress
- Assets primarily in illiquid form (property): may still qualify

2.5 Demographic Profile
- Age 18-25 (young adults entering workforce): eligible for training programs
- Age 55+ (approaching retirement): eligible for transitional support
- Recently divorced or widowed: eligible for emergency support
- New residents (less than 2 years): limited eligibility, case-by-case review

3. DECISION FRAMEWORK

3.1 Approval Criteria
An application is RECOMMENDED FOR APPROVAL when:
- Overall eligibility score is 55 or above (out of 100)
- At least 2 of 5 component scores are above 70
- No disqualifying factors are present (fraud indicators, false information)
- Income verification is consistent with declared information

3.2 Soft Decline Criteria
An application is RECOMMENDED FOR SOFT DECLINE when:
- Overall eligibility score is below 55
- Income and assets suggest financial self-sufficiency
- No immediate financial need is demonstrated
- Applicant may be redirected to economic enablement programs

3.3 Score Weighting
- Income Score: 30% weight
- Employment Score: 25% weight
- Wealth Score: 20% weight
- Family Score: 15% weight
- Demographic Score: 10% weight

4. ECONOMIC ENABLEMENT PROGRAMS

4.1 Upskilling Programs
- Digital literacy courses for job seekers
- Professional certification programs
- Language training (Arabic/English proficiency)
- Financial literacy workshops

4.2 Training Programs
- Vocational training in high-demand sectors
- Entrepreneurship development programs
- Sector-specific technical training
- Soft skills development workshops

4.3 Job Matching Services
- Resume building and career counseling
- Direct employer placement programs
- Government sector employment initiatives
- Private sector partnership placements

4.4 Career Counseling
- One-on-one career assessment sessions
- Industry transition guidance
- Skills gap analysis and development planning
- Long-term career pathway development

5. SUPPORT TIERS

Tier 1 - Emergency Support (Score 80+):
- Immediate financial assistance
- Priority housing support
- Full access to all enablement programs

Tier 2 - Standard Support (Score 55-79):
- Monthly financial assistance
- Access to training and upskilling programs
- Job matching services

Tier 3 - Enablement Only (Score 40-54):
- No direct financial assistance
- Full access to economic enablement programs
- Career counseling and job matching

Tier 4 - Self-Sufficient (Score below 40):
- Not eligible for financial support
- May access public enablement resources
- Redirected to community programs
